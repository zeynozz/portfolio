Investigation of the Accessibility Standards of ILIAS

  • Accessibility
  • WCAG 2.1
  • ARIA
  • Usability Testing

Abstract

Ensuring digital accessibility is a key requirement for equitable access to higher education, particularly for individuals with visual impairments. This thesis explores how accessible the ILIAS learning platform truly is by assessing both its compliance with the Web Content Acces- sibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1) and its practical usability for visually impaired users. A mixed- methods approach was applied, combining automated evaluation with the WAVE tool and empirical user testing. The latter incorporated eye tracking, screen reader interaction using JAWS, and concurrent think-aloud protocols. Participants with varying degrees of visual im- pairment were tasked with navigating course content and completing assessments within IL- IAS. The analysis revealed substantial gaps between formal compliance and functional ac- cessibility, including issues such as insufficient contrast ratios, non-semantic structures, inac- cessible interactive elements, and navigational inconsistencies. Efficiency metrics and System Usability Scale (SUS) results provided further evidence of usability constraints. The findings underscore the need for accessibility to be understood not merely as a technical checklist, but as a user-centred design imperative. Practical design recommendations are derived to support the development of more inclusive, barrier-free learning environments.

This work reflects the belief that accessibility is not an add-on, but a core value in the devel- opment of ethical, sustainable technology. It serves as a reminder that we, as designers and developers, carry the responsibility to create digital environments where everyone can partic- ipate equally – regardless of ability. To align with these principles, this thesis itself was format- ted and reviewed to meet accessibility standards, including screen reader compatibility and structural clarity. With this thesis, I hope to contribute to a digital future in which accessibility is viewed not as an optional feature, but as a core right that belongs to everyone.

I am honoured that this research was accepted for presentation at the AACE E-Learn World Conference 2025 in Bangkok, where it will contribute to the international dialogue on accessible and inclusive learning technologies. This recognition highlights the broader relevance of the study and underscores the importance of advancing accessibility in digital higher education worldwide.

What is PillBuddy?

Managing medications effectively is a significant challenge for elderly individuals, who often face difficulties due to complex schedules and age-related limitations. Many seniors require multiple medications with varying dosages, timings, and specific instructions, which can become overwhelming. Additionally, age-related memory loss or cognitive decline can lead to missed doses or incorrect medication intake, increasing health risks and complicating their medical care. Medication mismanagement can result in adverse health effects, including worsening symptoms, drug interactions, and increased risk of hospitalization. This not only impacts the individual’s health but also places additional strain on healthcare resources. PillBuddy aims to address these challenges by providing a reliable, easy-to-use platform that empowers elderly users to manage their medications independently. Through clear reminders, a straightforward interface, and options to monitor medication supply, PillBuddy enables seniors to maintain their routines more effectively, ensuring better adherence to prescribed therapies and enhancing overall quality of life.

What is ARth?

ARTH is an Augmented Reality learning concept designed to transform static school textbooks into interactive, engaging experiences. Instead of relying on flat diagrams and long text, students can scan markers in their books to unlock 3D models, animated Earth layers, interactive globes, and even gamified quizzes. The goal is to make learning subjects like geography more visual, dynamic, and fun while keeping students connected to their physical books. Using tools like Unity, Vuforia, and Cinema 4D, ARTH blends education with AR technology to create a multi-sensory, game-like approach to learning that improves engagement, motivation, and understanding.

Astro Breaker?

Astro Breaker is a space-themed Breakout game developed by Ilayda, Zeynep, and Mathani to combine learning and fun for students aged 10–14. Players destroy asteroids with an energy ball and solve math problems in addition, subtraction, or multiplication after each hit. Correct answers reward players with explosions and bonuses, while mistakes provide constructive feedback. Levels are colour-coded (green–addition, orange–subtraction, red–multiplication) with increasing difficulty. To boost engagement, the team integrated “game juice” such as visual effects, alien rewards, camera shake, and immersive sounds. While not yet classroom-tested, the game shows strong educational potential, with future updates planned for division, word puzzles, and multiplayer.

What is Journey?

Journey is a website for users that enjoy hiking and would like to discover more. They can document and share hikes they have undertaken and/or plan future hikes. While any user can search for hikes by submitting keywords, for example cities or areas, registered users have access to further services like uploading new hiking path entries and writing comments.
